## PAGE 1

**FADE IN:**

**EXT. RIVERBANK – NIGHT**  
*(optional setting)*  
Moonlight cuts through fog. The river moves like mercury, slow and indifferent. A figure — **JAMES (30s)** — watches the water ripple around his reflection.

> **JAMES (V.O.)**  
> They say the water remembers everything.  
> But I’ve learned that memory’s just another kind of drowning.

He lights a cigarette. Smoke curls upward, lost in the mist.

**CUT TO:**

**INT. BEDROOM – LATE NIGHT**  
*(alternate setting option)*  
The glow of a **computer monitor** spills across tangled sheets. JAMES stares at an unfinished message. The cursor blinks like a heartbeat.

> **JAMES (V.O.)**  
> Sleep’s a rumor. Sanity, a suggestion.

---

## PAGE 2

**EXT. CITY STREETS – NIGHT**

A rain-slick street under flickering neon. Cars hiss by. Somewhere, a jazz trumpet dies in an alley.

JAMES moves like a ghost among the living. The world hums. He doesn’t.

**SFX:** *Distant siren. Dripping gutters. The low thud of his heartbeat.*

> **JAMES (V.O.)**  
> There’s a moment every night when the city exhales —  
> and I swear it sounds like my name.

He stops under a sign: **“GUSHER FALLS MOTEL.”**

**CUT TO:**

**INT. MOTEL ROOM – NIGHT**

Peeling wallpaper. Buzzing neon outside.  
A woman’s silhouette sits on the bed — **MARIA (28)**, red lips and weary eyes.

> **MARIA**  
> You always come back to places that hate you.

> **JAMES**  
> Maybe I like consistency.

---

## PAGE 3

They watch each other. Too long. Too familiar.

> **MARIA**  
> You got what I asked for?

> **JAMES** *(smirks)*  
> Depends who’s asking — you, or the part of me that still gives a damn?

A beat. She reaches for a **photograph** on the nightstand. Two people smiling by a river. Torn down the middle.

> **MARIA**  
> We were happy once.  
> Before you drowned the best part of you.

JAMES looks away.

> **JAMES (V.O.)**  
> She talks like she’s from a memory I keep trying to delete.

---

## PAGE 4

**INT. MOTEL BATHROOM – NIGHT**

The mirror fogs. JAMES wipes it clean.  
For a moment, another face stares back — distorted, half-familiar.

> **JAMES (V.O.)**  
> That’s the trick.  
> You don’t lose your mind all at once.  
> It’s a slow exchange. Piece by piece.

He opens the cabinet — inside: **pills**, **razor**, **a folded note**.

He unfolds it.  
Written in shaky ink: **“STOP CALLING ME.”**

**SFX:** *Tap dripping rhythmically.*

**CUT TO:**

**EXT. RIVERBANK – NIGHT (FLASHBACK)**

Hands submerged.  
Water turns dark.  
A woman's **SCREAM** fades into the current.

---

## PAGE 5

**INT. MOTEL ROOM – LATER**

JAMES sits at the desk, laptop open.  
The cursor blinks over a new message:

> *Subject: Re: I saw her again.*

He hesitates. Starts typing.

> **JAMES (typing)**  
> She said she forgave me. But her eyes — they still blame me.

He deletes it all.

**Knock.**

> **JAMES**  
> Who is it?

> **VOICE (O.S.)**  
> Room service.

He didn’t order any.

**SFX:** *Knock again — louder.*

---

## PAGE 6

**INT. MOTEL ROOM – CONTINUOUS**

Door handle turns.  
JAMES reaches for something in the drawer — **a gun**, wrapped in a handkerchief.

> **JAMES (V.O.)**  
> Fear’s just faith pointed in the wrong direction.

The door creaks open —  
A **MAN IN A DARK COAT** steps in, face shadowed.

> **MAN**  
> You still pretending you didn’t see what happened?

> **JAMES**  
> Depends on who’s asking.

> **MAN**  
> Someone tired of cleaning up your ghosts.

A long silence.  
Rain hammers the window.

---

## PAGE 7

> **JAMES (V.O.)**  
> Truth is — we don’t get haunted by the dead.  
> We get haunted by the living parts we buried.

The MAN slides a **photo** across the table.  
The same river. A new body.

> **MAN**  
> She’s back.

> **JAMES**  
> That’s impossible.

> **MAN**  
> You said that last time.

**SFX:** *Thunder cracks.*

---

## PAGE 8

**EXT. RIVERBANK – NIGHT**

Rain sheets down.  
JAMES walks alone, drenched, flashlight trembling.

He reaches the same spot from the beginning — the reflection waits for him.

> **JAMES (V.O.)**  
> The city’s a mirror.  
> You don’t see what’s real — you see what you can stand.

He kneels, lowers the flashlight —  
And sees **her** face beneath the surface.

> **JAMES (V.O.)**  
> I should’ve known she’d come home.

---

## PAGE 9

**INT. BEDROOM – LATE NIGHT (PRESENT)**

JAMES jolts awake.  
The computer monitor glows. The same unsent message still blinks.

> **JAMES (V.O.)**  
> Maybe none of it happened.  
> Maybe all of it did.

He opens a drawer — the **photograph** is gone.

**SFX:** *Faint drip… from somewhere inside the room.*

He looks down — water pooling on the floor.  
He’s barefoot, and the reflection in the puddle isn’t his own.

---

## PAGE 10

**EXT. RIVERBANK – DAWN**

First light. Mist fades.

Detectives stand over a covered body.  
Flashbulbs. A coroner zips the bag.

One of them — the **MAN IN THE COAT** — lights a cigarette.

> **MAN**  
> Found her just like the others.  
> Always near the water.

He looks up — the river glints.  
For a second, a shape moves beneath the surface — impossible to name.

**FADE OUT.**

> *THE LAST STATIC*
